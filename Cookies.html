Understanding Cookies in Web Development

What are Cookies?

Cookies are small pieces of data stored on the user's device by the browser while they browse a website. They are used to remember information about the user, such as login details, preferences, or shopping cart contents. Cookies make the browsing experience more personalized and efficient.

How Cookies Work

When you visit a website, the server sends a cookie to your browser. The browser stores this cookie on your device and sends it back to the server every time you visit the same site. The server can then recognize the user and provide a customized experience.

Example Cookie Usage

For example: If you log into a website, a cookie might store your login session ID. The next time you visit, the site recognizes you and logs you in automatically.

Types of Cookies

There are two main types of cookies:

  • Session Cookies: These are temporary cookies that are erased when you close your browser.
  • Persistent Cookies: These cookies remain on your device until they expire or are deleted manually.

Setting Cookies in JavaScript

Here are 5 different cookies set using JavaScript:

document.cookie = "username=JohnDoe; expires=Fri, 31 Dec 2024 12:00:00 UTC; path=/";
document.cookie = "language=English; expires=Fri, 31 Dec 2024 12:00:00 UTC; path=/";
document.cookie = "theme=light; expires=Fri, 31 Dec 2024 12:00:00 UTC; path=/";
document.cookie = "country=Turkey; expires=Fri, 31 Dec 2024 12:00:00 UTC; path=/";
document.cookie = "sessionID=abc123; expires=Fri, 31 Dec 2024 12:00:00 UTC; path=/";

The above code sets five different cookies with the following information:

  • username: Stores the user's name as "JohnDoe".
  • language: Stores the preferred language as "English".
  • theme: Stores the theme preference as "light".
  • country: Stores the user's country as "Turkey".
  • sessionID: Stores a session ID for the user.

Viewing Cookies

You can view the cookies for a website using your browser's developer tools. Here's how to do it in most browsers:

  • Right-click on the page and select Inspect.
  • Go to the Application tab (in Chrome) or similar in other browsers.
  • Look for the Cookies section under Storage.
